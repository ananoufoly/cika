1 · How one month works

Walk a single turn. The pot is split three ways; the leftover builds a reserve that protects the circle and is placed with a bank treasury between payouts.

Discipline strictly wins. Leaving early is engineered to never pay — gains are clawed back to ≤ 0. The only winning strategy is to stay to the end.
The reserve does two jobs. It absorbs missed-payment shocks (solvency) and, while idle, is placed with the bank to generate a distribution shared with members.
Downside is capped. A member who stays can't lose more than a set floor, funded from the reserve. Exiters forfeit this protection.

3 · Who earns what

Per circle, and scaled across a portfolio. Reflects the current simulator settings.

Scaled to a portfolio

CIKA's revenue is a share of the treasury distribution (assets-under-management style) plus any auction fee — it scales with the number of circles, not by extracting from members' pots.

4 · Slot pricing — an open auction

We do not fix the price. Members bid for early access in an open auction; the price emerges from competition and reflects the time-value of receiving the lump sum months earlier (driven by ρ, members' cost of money). Early slots naturally bid more (they save the most waiting); the last slot bids ~0. The table below shows the expected bid per slot at the current ρ.

SlotImmediate (you get now)Asking price (bid)Deferred (unlocks at end)→ to reserveUnlock lagNet if you quit

Every winner takes the same immediate slice (half the pot). The asking price (bid) is subtracted from your own deferred half and flows into the reserve. Early slots cost more (you pay to go first); the last slot pays least and keeps its full deferred. "Net if you quit" = 0 at every slot — the deferred half forfeits on exit, so taking-and-running never beats staying.
